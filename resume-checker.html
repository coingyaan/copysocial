What is ATS and How Does It Affect Your Resume?

ATS stands for Applicant Tracking System. It's software that companies use to automatically scan, parse, and rank resumes before human recruiters see them.

How ATS Resume Scanning Works:

  1. Upload: You submit your resume through a job application portal
  2. Parsing: ATS software extracts text and data from your resume
  3. Keyword Matching: The system compares your resume against job description keywords
  4. Scoring: Your resume gets an ATS compatibility score based on match percentage
  5. Ranking: Resumes are ranked from highest to lowest score
  6. Filtering: Only top-scoring resumes reach human recruiters

Common ATS Rejection Reasons:

  • Using images, graphics, or tables (ATS can't read them)
  • Fancy fonts or unusual formatting
  • Missing critical keywords from the job description
  • Incorrect section headings (like "Experience" vs "Work History")
  • Headers and footers (ATS often skips these)
  • Multiple columns or complex layouts

That's why an ATS resume checker is essential. It shows you exactly what ATS systems see and what might cause rejection.

How do I know if my resume is ATS friendly?

What file formats work with ATS systems?

The best ATS-friendly file formats are PDF and DOCX (Microsoft Word). Avoid older formats like DOC, images (JPG, PNG), or plain text files. Our resume checker accepts both PDF and DOCX formats. Always use simple formatting without tables, text boxes, or images for maximum ATS compatibility.

How can I make my resume more ATS friendly?

To make your resume ATS-friendly: (1) Use standard section headings like "Work Experience" and "Education", (2) Include keywords from the job description, (3) Use simple formatting with no images or graphics, (4) Avoid tables and text boxes, (5) Use standard fonts, (6) Save as PDF or DOCX, (7) Include measurable achievements with numbers. Our ATS checker gives you specific recommendations for your resume.

Can ATS read PDF resumes?

Yes, modern ATS systems can read PDF resumes created from Word or other text-based programs. However, PDFs created from scanned images or with complex formatting may not parse correctly. Always use a text-based PDF and test it with our ATS checker to ensure compatibility.

How long should an ATS-friendly resume be?

For most professionals, a 1-2 page ATS-friendly resume is ideal. Entry-level candidates should aim for 1 page, mid-level professionals 1-2 pages, and senior executives up to 2 pages. ATS systems can handle longer resumes, but recruiters prefer concise resumes that highlight relevant experience and achievements.

Do I need to customize my resume for each job?

Yes! Customizing your resume for each job application significantly increases your chances of passing ATS screening. Include specific keywords from the job description, highlight relevant experience, and adjust your skills section to match the job requirements. Use our ATS checker to optimize each version.
